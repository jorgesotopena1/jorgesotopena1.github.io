b-Jet and c-Jet Identification at LHCb Using Deep Learning Techniques

The goal of my project was to improve a deep learning algorithm identifying subatomic particles from the Large Hadron Collider beauty (LHCb) experiment at CERN. I analyzed newly simulated data based off recent LHCb conditions and considered several input data configurations in order to improve the deep learning algorithm.

Identifying M Dwarfs and their Stellar Companions

I studied the environment of M dwarf stars to assess the number of close-orbit stellar companions. I developed an efficient method to analyze 800 images of stars and used this to characterize binary systems based on distance and color. I discovered ten new binary candidates, providing important constraints for M dwarf exoplanet research.

Thermalization effects in late-time Type Ia Supernova light curves

The goal of this project is to understand why certain "twin" Supernovae (SNe)-two distinct SNe whose light curves and spectra are nearly identical immediately after explosion-differ in luminosity several hundred days later. We studied this discrepancy by simulating how radioactive decay produces heat in the ejecta.
